# CollabStudio

CollabStudio provides dedicated spaces for collaboration, designed to support both research and educational use cases. Within a CollabStudio, users work together as groups to share resources and develop workspaces collaboratively.

Each group is allocated 5 GB of shared storage on our main JupyterHub service to support their projects. Please make sure to review the PVC (Persistent Volume Claim) policy on [this page](../policies/pvc-policy.md) for important details regarding storage usage and retention.

Supported CollabStudios are:

- **Project**: Projects are collaborative spaces designed for research groups to develop, organize, and share their work. Within a project, members can create and manage multiple workspaces, allowing them to build complete end-to-end pipelines. To create your first project, follow this [tutorial](../collabstudio/create-project.md).
- **Classroom**: Classrooms allows instructors to conduct their courses within NDP. Classrooms incorporate multiple modules and allow students to be assigned to groups for collaborative work. To help you create your first classroom, [review this page](../collabstudio/create-classroom.md).
- **Data Challenges**: Data Challenges provide an opportunity to tackle real-world problems using advanced datasets, computational tools, and AI-driven methods. To help you create your first data challenge, [review this page](../collabstudio/hosting-a-data-challenge.md)

**NOTE:** Both Classroom and Data Challenge are CollabStudios restricted to users with *Educator* credentials. To request such credentials, [contact the NDP team](../contact.md).
